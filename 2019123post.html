A Little Advice

Yesterday I spoke with a top notch developer. This was a meeting I’ve been hoping would happen for months.

It turns out a gentleman at my gym is developer from way back. He’s written a definitive guide to a particular language, worked at NeXT, and started his own highly successful training organization for current and future developers.

I was nervous to say the least.

But after the workout was through I sucked it up and introduced myself, explaining that I was diving into the world of software development and asked if he had any tips or recommendations.

He had three big ones.

  1. Keep going. Even when you don’t understand it all just keep moving.
  2. Everyone, even at his level, encounter challenges. Just that morning he was working on a bit of code to clean up degraded images. He said he didn’t even know where to begin. But he found a thread and started pulling on it. By the end of the day he had the code completed.
  3. Get enough sleep.

Anyone that knows me knows the sleep struggles I’ve overcome and how seriously I take my sleep. One item on the list checked off!

As for the other two, it was encouraging to hear from someone with so much experience that the trail to competency and eventually mastery is well worn and familiar. There are times when trying to complete an assignment that I feel incapable and that others must be sailing through.
