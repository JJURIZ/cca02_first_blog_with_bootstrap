Pulling on the Bootstraps

I recall listening to a book which briefly described the origin of the phrase “to pull oneself up by your bootstraps”. I don’t recall the details but I do remember the original intent; to undertake an impossible task. It is, after all, impossible to pull oneself up by their bootstraps. The meaning has switched poles, now indicating something that can be done through self-determination and applied effort.

When it comes to the Bootstrap framework, another phrase comes to mind; “out of the frying pan and into the fire”. Creating the original blog site assignment without a framework was difficult yet exhilarating. The end results resemble a hand crafted piece, lovable only by the creator. Bootstrap is on it’s way to change this, making the assigned site appear polished and clean.

There is a catch though. Bootstrap isn’t a decal I can throw on to cover up the imperfections. It appears to me to be its own universe, a morass of classes to tweak the design and layout of a site. The HTML which seemed so straightforward before has become a queue for Bootstrap’s identifiers.

I’m looking forward to learning about Bootstrap but I wonder how much I’ll capture before moving on to the next topic. It seems that rather than learning the material I’m learning where to find the material for later use.

Is this an impossible task or a lot of effort? I suspect it’s a blend of both. I’ll put in the effort but to “learn” Bootstrap, to be familiar with all its classes, seems impossible to me.
