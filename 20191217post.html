In Memory

Hello. This is a dispatch from the unknown.

It’s a place I will never leave, though I hope to make some discoveries while here, especially in the realm of computer programming. appear polished and clean.

I write this as a reminder to my future self. Whether it will be a reminder of accomplishment or failure remains to be seen. At the moment I am incredulous with myself. You see I’ve been looking at JavaScript code for several months now and still find myself semi-literate. I can read and follow these beginner lessons but I’ll be damned if I can retrieve them from memory.

At the moment I’m reviewing and re-reviewing a JavaScript refresher video which concludes with the creation of a basic form that takes two text inputs and displays them on the screen. Straightforward. Simple. Seen it a hundred times at this point. And yet, when I attempt to recreate from memory I choke. I can declare my variables without issue, including the array into which the books will be stored. And that’s about it. The rest of the code, about 12 lines worth, are hazy, like a face I’ve seen once and just encountered again. It’s insulting, even embarrassing. Because this isn’t something I’ve only run into once. It’s as if when I’m out of the office I don’t recognize someone I see every working day. Of all the battles learning to code, this is the one that concerns me the most. If I can’t drag these few simple lines of code out of the shackles of my mind, how am I ever going to do this professionally?

As of yet I’m not giving up. If the concepts were as nebulous as the details I would fret and seriously consider looking at something else, like plumbing. The concepts once grappled with make sense and provide the kind of mental stimulation I look for in and out of work.

Here is my marker, my stake in the ground. I hope to look back in a month and marvel at how easy it is to reproduce this code, shifting my latent concerns to some bigger and more worthy dilema.
